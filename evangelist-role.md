I've been a founder three times. I know what it takes to go from zero to one — the resource constraints, the technical debt decisions, the audience-building that happens alongside product-building. I also know that for most startups, AI isn't a feature. It's a new way of building entirely. The question is how to make that shift practical, not theoretical.

I'm already building in the Anthropic ecosystem. At Kelp.digital, I built a custom MCP server to integrate Claude into our content rights platform. That's 44,000 lines of TypeScript I wrote myself, and the MCP architecture taught me firsthand how Claude extends beyond chat into agentic workflows. I've developed AI plugins, written SDKs for third-party developers, and presented those systems at conferences. I know the developer's perspective because I am one.

At Simstore (part of Ipsos), I lead AI adoption across the technology team — writing guidelines, building internal agents, creating documentation, and serving as the internal evangelist for engineers skeptical or unsure where to start. That experience taught me that adoption isn't about convincing. It's about demonstrating. Show a team one workflow that saves them hours, and the skepticism dissolves.

I've also written successful grant applications for Web3 Foundation, Parity Technologies, and the EU Next Generation Internet program. That's stakeholder development at its most concrete: translating technical vision into outcomes that funders and partners can believe in.

As a digital nomad since 2015, I've worked across startups in Finland, Sweden, and globally. I'm comfortable in rooms where the energy is scrappy and the stakes are high. That's the startup world. I've lived it.

What draws me to this role is the intersection: Anthropic's technology is genuinely novel — model-level safety as a differentiator, extended thinking for complex reasoning, the MCP protocol as an open standard. My background lets me speak to founders not as a cheerleader, but as someone who's built on the platform, led adoption in a large organization, and understands the startup journey from the inside.

I want to help developers and founders discover what Claude makes possible — not through hype, but through demos they can run, tutorials they can follow, and a community they can belong to.
