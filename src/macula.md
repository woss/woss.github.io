## Job role

I'm Daniel Maricic. Let me tell you about Macula — it's where I proved I could carry a product from zero to production as a solo founder, owning every layer from infrastructure to user experience. Macula is a content rights management platform that evolved from my earlier blockchain work on Anagolay Network. It gives photographers and creators the tools to store, manage, verify, and license their work using legally binding digital certificates — without needing to understand cryptography or blockchain.

As Founder and CTO, I designed and built the entire platform from scratch. I personally wrote 44,000 lines of production TypeScript covering the full stack: Svelte frontend, serverless backend, REST and GraphQL APIs, a Lightroom plugin, and a custom MCP server that extends the Claude AI ecosystem. The MCP server is particularly significant — it enables AI agents to interact directly with Macula's content rights data, positioning the platform at the intersection of copyright management and AI.

The architecture is event-driven with dual IPFS/S3 storage for content persistence and retrieval. The image processing pipeline is built on Sharp and ExifTool — handling everything from resizing and format conversion to EXIF metadata extraction and preservation. For analytics, I integrated QuestDB for real-time metrics on content usage and SurrealDB for knowledge graph queries that connect creators, licenses, and media assets in a traversable graph.

I secured grant funding from the European Commission's Next Generation Internet initiative — writing successful proposals, managing milestone reporting, and building relationships with EU program officers. The grant process required articulating technical vision to non-technical stakeholders, a skill that shaped how I communicate about the platform.

This role was 100% remote, where I practiced async communication, self-managed delivery, and cross-timezone collaboration daily.
