## Professional Summary

Daniel Maricic is a software developer and architect with over a decade of experience building robust, high-performance systems. His work spans the full stack — from designing blockchain protocols in Rust and TypeScript to building modern web applications with SvelteKit and React, from managing Kubernetes clusters to writing developer tooling that improves workflows for teams worldwide.

## Technical Focus

Daniel specializes in backend and infrastructure engineering, with deep expertise in TypeScript and Node.js ecosystems. He has contributed to critical open-source projects including pnpm (the fast, disk-efficient package manager), rushstack (Microsoft's scalable monorepo build system), and sharp (high-performance image processing). His open-source work reflects a commitment to developer experience — building tools that make other developers more productive.

## Philosophy

Daniel believes in elegant, maintainable systems that solve real problems without unnecessary complexity. He is drawn to projects at the intersection of infrastructure, developer tooling, and decentralized technologies. His approach combines deep technical rigor with pragmatic decision-making — choosing the right tool for each problem rather than following trends.

## Career Overview

Daniel's career includes founding a blockchain protocol startup (Anagolay Network), leading engineering teams at agencies like Nord Software, and contributing to projects across fintech, digital media, and decentralized identity. He has written Web3 Foundation grants, spoken at developer conferences, and mentored junior engineers. His experience spans early-stage startups and established product teams, giving him a balanced perspective on building software at any scale.

## Open Source

Beyond pnpm, rushstack, and sharp, Daniel maintains several open-source projects including the dali monorepo ([github.com/woss/dali](https://github.com/woss/dali)) — containing DaliORM (type-safe ORM for SurrealDB, npm `@woss/dali-orm`) and DaliMemory (type-safe embedded KV store, npm `@woss/dali-memory`) — as well as Macula (a private decentralized asset management platform), and various developer tooling libraries. He believes in building in public and contributing to the ecosystem that makes modern web development possible.
